Let's dive into three powerful Stoic practices you can start using today. 

First, the practice of 'Negative Visualization'. This involves consciously imagining worst-case scenarios to build emotional resilience. By confronting fears in a controlled manner, you can reduce their power over you. Picture yourself calmly handling a difficult situation, and notice how this shifts your perspective.

Second, the 'Dichotomy of Control'. Focus only on what you can control—your thoughts, actions, and responses. Let go of everything else. By redirecting your energy towards controllable aspects, you free yourself from unnecessary stress.

Third, 'Mindful Journaling'. Each evening, reflect on your day by writing down three things you did well and one area for improvement. This practice fosters self-awareness and continuous growth, allowing you to approach each day with clarity and purpose.
